About me

My full name is Muhammad Fahmi Fauzi, a master's graduate in theoretical physics and currently a research assistant at the Theoretical Astrophysics and Nuclear-Particle Physics Group, University of Indonesia. I work under my master's supervisors: Prof. Anto Sulaksono and Dr. Handhika S. Ramadhan, focusing on the observational and phenomenological aspects of gravitating objects.

Research stuff

In general, I attempt to connect various theoretical models of black holes and (ultra-)compact objects in general relativity and other theories of gravity with observations. Most of my recent work is focused on optical observables, but I think the gravitational-wave channel has started to gain my attention.

Pict: a destroyed spinning nonsingular black hole surrounded by a thin accretion disk [Fauzi et al. CQG 42, 225012 (2025)]

Research interests
  1. Observational aspects of black hole destruction (mainly curious about this one),
  2. Optical observables of various black hole models (shadows, lensing, appearance, etc.),
  3. Gravitational waves (quasinormal modes, echoes, etc.),
  4. Stuff related to (ultra-)compact objects
